# Fuck it, we ball (04/13/2026)

There's a three-point shooter who shoots 50% from three. National championship game. Down to the last shot. He's missed his last ten attempts.

The coach asks, "Who's taking it?"

"Fuck it, I'll shoot it."

"You're our best shooter. Let's get it."

He shoots. Makes it. They win.

---

Afterward, a reporter catches him: "How did you know? Why were you so confident taking that shot after missing ten straight?"

"Statistically," he says, "after I miss the first shot, I'm more likely to make the next one. After missing ten in a row, I'm *way* more likely to make the next one. 1 - 0.5^10 percentage."

The reporter pauses. "Does that mean if you make ten shots in a row, you're more likely to miss the next one?"

"Fuck no."

---

The moral of the story is this: **no matter what the situation is, he always believes he has an advantage.**

It's bullshit math. Everyone knows it's bullshit math. He probably knows it's bullshit math. But the logic doesn't matter. The *belief* does.

When you're 0 for 10, you tell yourself regression to the mean is coming. When you're 10 for 10, you're hot and the streak continues. Either way, you're the one taking the shot.

That's the advantage.

---

He tries to apply that mentality to life: whatever decision comes up, whatever situation unfolds, act in a way that will eventually pay off. Make the thing you need to happen *happen*. Don't spend time wondering what the right decision is—make the irreversible decision and **make it right** instead.

Because confidence doesn't come from having good odds. Good odds come from having confidence.

The shooter knows this. That's why when everyone else sees ten missed shots, he sees one made shot away from winning. That's why he says "fuck it" and takes it anyway.

He's not playing probability. He's playing certainty. And in his hands, the ball goes in.
