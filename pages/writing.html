Writing

~ Long

bread arrives
Bread arrives with every order.
Unannounced, without asking to be noticed —
the way certain things arrive.
It sits at the edge of the bowl,
patient as something that knows.
Plain. Tender. My fork moves around it,
past it, through it.
I throw it away thinking about the future.
I always did.
There is a light left on in another room.
I don't remember leaving it.
I don't turn it off.
Sometimes I go looking for May —
something strawberry-warm,
a scent that didn't travel far.
You had to be chosen to catch it.
I know I won't find it.
I go looking anyway.
That's the kind of person I am.
But the bowl is still here.
And the bread arrives,
the way it always has —
the way a coat on a hook is there,
the way a table set for people you love is there.
I eat the bread now.
I sit with it.
It doesn't taste like much.
It tastes like everything.
